What Your Son Will Learn in Quran Classes

When enrolling your child in a Quran course, you can expect a structured program designed for gradual progress. Typical lessons include:

  1. Arabic Alphabet and Pronunciation: Learning to recognize and pronounce Arabic letters correctly.

  2. Basic Quran Reading: Understanding how letters connect to form words and verses.

  3. Tajweed Rules: Applying correct articulation and rhythm while reciting.

  4. Memorization (Hifz): Step-by-step memorization of short surahs, building toward larger chapters.

  5. Tafseer and Islamic Manners: Learning meanings, stories, and morals from the Quran.

With consistent practice, your son will grow confident in reciting and understanding the Quran.

How to Keep Your Son Motivated

Learning the Quran should never feel like a burden. Children learn best when they feel inspired, supported, and appreciated. Here are some ways to make quran lessons for my son effective and enjoyable:

  • Celebrate small achievements: Reward your son for memorizing new verses.

  • Create a routine: Set aside specific times for Quran study each day.

  • Be involved: Sit with him during lessons to show interest and encouragement.

  • Share stories from the Quran: Relate what he learns to real-life lessons.

  • Lead by example: Let him see you reading and reflecting on the Quran too.

When the Quran becomes part of family life, learning feels natural and joyful.
